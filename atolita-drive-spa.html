Overview & Our Guiding Principles

Welcome to Atolita's interactive guide for organizing our Google Drive! This guide is designed to help you easily understand and effectively use our new file management system. By following these guidelines, you'll contribute to a more organized, secure, and efficient workspace for everyone. Explore the sections to learn about our structure, how permissions work, and best practices for your daily tasks.

Our Guiding Principles for Google Drive Organization

  • Centralization: All team-owned content lives in Shared Drives. This ensures company data is safe and accessible, even if team members change.
  • Logical Hierarchy: Our folders are organized in a clear, intuitive way that mirrors our teams and projects, making it easy to find what you need.
  • Granular Permissions: You'll have access to exactly what you need for your role, and nothing more. This keeps our information secure.
  • Consistency: We use consistent naming and structures across all drives and folders to avoid confusion.
  • Dedicated External Sharing: We have specific, controlled areas for sharing with people outside Atolita, keeping our internal drives secure.
  • Regular Review: We'll periodically review our system to ensure it remains effective and secure for everyone.

Understanding Your Access & Permissions

Understanding permissions is key to working effectively and securely in Google Drive. This section explains the different access levels you might encounter and how they apply to Shared Drives, folders, and individual files.

1. Shared Drive Access Levels

These roles determine what you can do within an entire Shared Drive:

Manager Role

Can manage members, content, and settings of the Shared Drive. (Typically for Department Heads or IT Admins).

Content Manager Role

Can add, edit, move, and delete content. (Often for Team Leads or Senior Staff managing content).

Contributor Role

Can add and edit content. This is the common role for most team members actively working within a drive.

Commenter Role

Can only comment on files. Useful if you need to provide feedback without editing.

Viewer Role

Can only view files. This is for general access to resources or documents you only need to read.

Your Access: You will typically have "Content Manager" or "Contributor" access to your own departmental Shared Drive. For other relevant Shared Drives (like Company Hub or cross-functional projects), you'll likely have "Viewer" or "Commenter" access.

2. Folder & File Level Permissions

  • Inheritance: Permissions are usually inherited from the Shared Drive or parent folder.
  • Specific Access: Sometimes, a specific folder (especially for confidential information) or individual file might have more restricted access than the rest of the drive.
  • External Sharing: When we share documents with external partners (clients, vendors), we always use the dedicated `Atolita - External Collaboration` Shared Drive. If you need to share something externally, please ensure it's placed there first, and only grant the minimum necessary permissions (e.g., "Viewer" for general documents, "Commenter" for review, "Editor" only if they need to actively contribute). **Never share entire departmental Shared Drives externally.**

Best Practices & Getting Started

To make our Google Drive system work efficiently for everyone, please follow these best practices. This section also outlines how you can get started with the new structure.

Best Practices for Files & Folders

  • File Naming: Use consistent file naming for easy identification and searching. A good practice is: YYYYMMDD_ProjectName_DocumentTitle_Version.ext (e.g., 20250521_Q2Campaign_MarketingPlan_V2.docx)
  • Folder Naming: Use clear, descriptive names for any new folders you create within the established structure. The main departmental drives are numbered (e.g., `01_Executive`) for easy sorting.
  • "My Drive" Usage: Your personal "My Drive" should be used only for:
    • Truly personal, non-company-related files.
    • Temporary working files or drafts that you will eventually move to the appropriate Shared Drive once they are ready for team collaboration or finalization. **All company-owned work should eventually reside in a Shared Drive.**
